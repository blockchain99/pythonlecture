<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regular_season">Regular season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_all-time_NFL_win%E2%80%93loss_records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Regular season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pete_Rozelle_and_George_Halas.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Pete_Rozelle_and_George_Halas.jpg/220px-Pete_Rozelle_and_George_Halas.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="155" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Pete_Rozelle_and_George_Halas.jpg/330px-Pete_Rozelle_and_George_Halas.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Pete_Rozelle_and_George_Halas.jpg/440px-Pete_Rozelle_and_George_Halas.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2651" data-file-height="1869" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pete_Rozelle_and_George_Halas.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/George_Halas" title="George Halas">George Halas</a> (right) of the Chicago Bears, who has recorded the most regular season wins.</div></div></div>
<p>The following is a listing of all 32 current <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> (NFL) teams ranked by their regular season win–loss record percentage, accurate as of the end of the <a href="/wiki/2018_NFL_season" title="2018 NFL season">2018 NFL regular season</a>. For teams that previously played in the <a href="/wiki/All-America_Football_Conference" title="All-America Football Conference">All-America Football Conference</a> (AAFC), their AAFC win-loss records are not included as the NFL does not officially count AAFC statistics despite the 1950 NFL–AAFC merger.<sup id="cite_ref-AAFCstats_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AAFCstats-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> However, for teams that played in the <a href="/wiki/American_Football_League" title="American Football League">American Football League</a> (AFL), their AFL win-loss records are included and officially counted by the NFL. Ties are registered as a half win and a half loss when calculating the win-loss record.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_all-time_NFL_win%E2%80%93loss_records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: lower-alpha;">
<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">
<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">For example, the Browns' regular season win–loss record does not include a 47–4–3 record compiled in the <a href="/wiki/All-America_Football_Conference" title="All-America Football Conference">All-America Football Conference</a> from 1932 to 1954, nor does their playoff record include the 5–0 record compiled during the same period. Likewise, the 49ers' AAFC regular season win–loss record of 38–14–2, nor their 1–1 ASAFC playoff record are officially counted</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-Patriots-5"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Patriots_5-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The Patriots played as the "Boston Patriots" during <a href="/wiki/1960_Boston_Patriots_season" title="1960 Boston Patriots season">1960</a>–<a href="/wiki/1970_Boston_Patriots_season" title="1970 Boston Patriots season">1970</a>, before adopting the "New England Patriots" name, which they have played under since the <a href="/wiki/1971_New_England_Patriots" class="mw-redirect" title="1971 New England Patriots">1971 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The team was also briefly known as the "Bay State Patriots" prior to the 1971 season.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-Colts-7"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Colts_7-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The Colts played under the "<a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Baltimore_Colts" title="History of the Baltimore Colts">Baltimore Colts</a>" name during <a href="/wiki/1953_Baltimore_Colts_season" title="1953 Baltimore Colts season">1953</a>–<a href="/wiki/1983_Baltimore_Colts" class="mw-redirect" title="1983 Baltimore Colts">1983</a>, before relocating to Indianapolis and adopting their current "Indianapolis Colts" name, which they have played under since the <a href="/wiki/1984_Indianapolis_Colts" class="mw-redirect" title="1984 Indianapolis Colts">1984 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-Chargers-8"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Chargers_8-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The Chargers played as the "Los Angeles Chargers" in <a href="/wiki/1960_Los_Angeles_Chargers_season" title="1960 Los Angeles Chargers season">1960</a> before relocating to San Diego in <a href="/wiki/1961_San_Diego_Chargers_season" title="1961 San Diego Chargers season">1961</a> and adopting the "San Diego Chargers" as their name during <a href="/wiki/1961_San_Diego_Chargers_season" title="1961 San Diego Chargers season">1961</a>–<a href="/wiki/2016_San_Diego_Chargers_season" title="2016 San Diego Chargers season">2016</a>. Before the <a href="/wiki/2017_Los_Angeles_Chargers_season" title="2017 Los Angeles Chargers season">2017 season</a>, the franchise relocated back to Los Angeles and has played under their initial "Los Angeles Chargers" name since.</span>
</li>
